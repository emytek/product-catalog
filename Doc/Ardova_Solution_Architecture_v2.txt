1.0   EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

This document establishes the definitive solution architecture for the Ardova
Trade and Supply Management System, implemented entirely on SAP S/4HANA Public
Cloud Edition. It supersedes all prior architectural drafts and reflects the
decisions reached during the solution design workshops conducted with Ardova's
Trading Desk, Trade Finance, and Trade Operations leadership.

The system addresses a commercially critical gap in Ardova's current operating
model: the absence of a single, integrated platform that connects the negotiation
of petroleum cargo contracts with the financial instruments that fund those
contracts and the logistics operations that deliver them. Today, these three
functions operate with significant information latency between them — a condition
that introduces pricing risk, operational delays, and an inability to monitor
the company's real-time trading position with any degree of confidence.

The architecture adopted here is built around three purposefully designed
components, each mapped to an existing or extensible capability within
S/4HANA Public Cloud:

  (i)   A Custom Business Object, referred to throughout this document as the
        Trade Card, serves as the master record for every trade transaction.
        It is created at the moment a contract is agreed at the Trading Desk
        and assigned a unique transaction identifier in the format PM/YYYY/NNN.
        This identifier becomes the common reference across all three components.

  (ii)  The Treasury Management module, native to S/4HANA Public Cloud, is
        configured and extended to handle all Trade Finance activities — from
        Form M application through to Letter of Credit issuance, Bills for
        Collection, Bank Guarantee management, and cargo insurance documentation.
        Users access Trade Finance by entering the Trade Card ID, which
        immediately surfaces the relevant contract data without re-entry.

  (iii) The Transportation Management module, equally native to S/4HANA Public
        Cloud, is configured and extended to manage Trade Operations — covering
        vessel deployment, cargo movement, service provider coordination,
        regulatory notifications, multi-point volume capture, and a real-time
        view of the trading position for each active transaction.

The system is designed in strict alignment with SAP's Clean Core principle,
meaning all extensions are implemented through the designated extensibility
channels provided by S/4HANA Public Cloud — Key User Extensibility for field
and logic additions to standard objects, and SAP Business Technology Platform
(BTP) for analytics, workflow automation, and any capability that sits outside
the core system. No modifications are made to standard SAP code.

2.1   Platform Declaration
────────────────────────────
This document applies exclusively to SAP S/4HANA Public Cloud Edition.

All architectural decisions, configuration approaches, extensibility mechanisms,
and integration patterns described herein are valid for and restricted to the
Public Cloud edition of S/4HANA. Any concept, transaction code, configuration
path, or development approach that is exclusive to SAP S/4HANA On-Premise,
SAP ECC, or SAP S/4HANA Private Cloud has been deliberately excluded.

The distinction matters for the following reasons:

  —  S/4HANA Public Cloud enforces a strictly managed extensibility model.
     Customers extend the system through approved channels only: the Custom
     Business Objects application, the Custom Fields and Logic application,
     and side-by-side extensions on SAP BTP. Direct modification of SAP
     standard objects, programs, or database structures is neither permitted
     nor possible in the Public Cloud edition.

  —  System configuration in S/4HANA Public Cloud is performed through
     Fiori-based configuration applications accessed within the system,
     primarily through the "Configure Your Solution" application and the
     relevant module setup apps. There is no direct access to the traditional
     ABAP customising environment (IMG) as an open development tool.

  —  All custom user interfaces are built as SAPUI5 applications deployed on
     SAP BTP Cloud Foundry, consuming OData services exposed by the S/4HANA
     system. They are not developed as additions to the core system itself.

  —  Workflow and business process automation is handled through SAP Build
     Process Automation on BTP — not through any classical SAP workflow engine.

3.0   ARCHITECTURAL PHILOSOPHY — CLEAN CORE ON S/4HANA PUBLIC CLOUD
================================================================================

3.1   What Clean Core Means for this Implementation
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SAP's Clean Core principle, which is foundational to the Public Cloud edition,
establishes a clear contract between the customer and the platform: the standard
system remains untouched and upgradeable, while customer-specific requirements
are addressed through structured extensibility. For Ardova, this means every
customisation in this solution has a defined home:

  Requirement Type                  Extensibility Approach
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  New business object (Trade Card)  Custom Business Objects application
  Additional fields on standard     Custom Fields and Logic application
  objects (TRM, TM)                 (Key User Extensibility)
  Custom business logic on CBOs     Custom Logic within the CBO application
  Workflow and approvals            SAP Build Process Automation (BTP)
  Custom Fiori applications         SAPUI5 apps deployed on SAP BTP CF
  Real-time analytics               SAP Analytics Cloud, embedded in BTP apps
  System integration                SAP Integration Suite (BTP)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3.2   Implications for Long-Term Supportability
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
By adhering strictly to these extensibility channels, the Ardova implementation
maintains a system that can receive SAP quarterly updates without the risk of
custom code being overwritten or broken. This is not merely a theoretical benefit
— in practice, it means the Ardova team can stay current with S/4HANA Public
Cloud innovations (new analytical capabilities, updated module features, security
patches) without a costly upgrade project each time. For a trading business
operating in a volatile commodity environment, the ability to adopt new platform
capabilities quickly represents a genuine competitive consideration.

3.3   The Principle of Standard Before Custom
───────────────────────────────────────────────
Throughout this design, every requirement was first evaluated against available
standard functionality. Custom development was introduced only where no standard
configuration path could meet the requirement within an acceptable tolerance.
The result is a solution where the core transaction and financial logic sits in
standard SAP modules (TRM and TM), while the Trade Card CBO provides the
connective layer and master reference that binds them together. The effort and
risk of custom development is therefore confined to where it genuinely adds
value — not distributed across the system as a default approach.

4.2   Transaction Lifecycle
─────────────────────────────
Every trade transaction in this system moves through a defined sequence. The
sequence is enforced through status management on the Trade Card CBO, and it
reflects the actual operational reality of how Ardova's business functions:

  Stage 1 — Contract Origination (Trading Desk)
    The Trading Desk creates a Trade Card in the Custom Business Objects app.
    All key commercial parameters are recorded: product type, supplier,
    contracted volume, pricing type, delivery terms, and Laycan window. On
    saving the record, the system assigns the unique transaction ID in the
    format PM/YYYY/NNN. From this point, the transaction ID is the universal
    reference across all activities.

  Stage 2 — Trade Finance (Treasury Management)
    The Trade Finance team opens the Treasury Management application and enters
    the transaction ID. The system surfaces all contract data from the Trade Card
    immediately. The Finance team then proceeds to establish the appropriate
    payment instrument (LC, Bank Guarantee, Bills for Collection, or Advance
    Payment), apply for Form M where required, obtain insurance coverage, and
    manage all associated documentary processes. Completion of this stage and its
    approval gate is recorded against the Trade Card status.

  Stage 3 — Trade Operations (Transportation Management)
    The Trade Operations team opens the Transportation Management application and
    enters the same transaction ID. Contract and finance data already recorded
    against this ID are displayed. The Operations team maintains vessel
    information, nominates service providers, manages regulatory notifications,
    records all volume measurements across the cargo journey, and monitors the
    real-time trading position. Final cargo closure, once all costs are confirmed
    and reconciled, is executed here and posts the transaction to FI/CO.

  Note on Parallelism: While the standard process sequence places Trade Finance
  before Trade Operations, the system does not technically prevent the Operations
  team from beginning their data entry in parallel. In practice, certain
  operational activities — particularly vessel positioning and agent nomination —
  must commence well before Finance documentation is complete, given shipping
  lead times. The architecture accommodates this operational reality while
  preserving the Finance approval gate as a precondition for final cargo closure.

5.0   COMPONENT 1 — TRADE CARD (CUSTOM BUSINESS OBJECT)
================================================================================

5.1   Design Rationale
────────────────────────
The Trade Card is the architectural cornerstone of this solution. In S/4HANA
Public Cloud, the appropriate and supported mechanism for creating a net-new
business object — one that does not exist in any standard module — is the Custom
Business Objects application, available in the Fiori Launchpad. This application
provides a structured, upgrade-safe way to define a new data object with its own
fields, business logic, user interface, and number determination, all without
modifying any standard SAP object.

The Trade Card is deliberately designed to be a summary and master record rather
than a data-entry-heavy screen. Its purpose is to capture the essential contract
parameters, generate the unique transaction ID, and provide the reference that
all downstream activity will anchor to. The detailed functional work — the
specifics of the LC terms, the vessel scheduling, the volume measurements — lives
in the respective standard module applications.

5.2   Creating the Trade Card in the Custom Business Objects Application
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In S/4HANA Public Cloud, the Custom Business Objects application is accessed
directly from the Fiori Launchpad. A Key User or an administrator with the
appropriate business role opens this application and creates the object through
a guided interface. No development environment, no programming IDE, and no
ABAP workbench access is required or appropriate for this step.

The object creation process in the application involves:
  —  Defining the object name (Trade Card) and its technical identifier
  —  Configuring number determination (the PM/YYYY/NNN sequence)
  —  Defining all field nodes (the Trade Card header with its field list)
  —  Configuring the generated UI (which fields appear, in what order)
  —  Publishing the object to make it available to users

5.4   Unique Transaction ID — Number Determination
────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The transaction ID format PM/YYYY/NNN is configured within the Custom Business
Objects application under the number determination settings of the Trade Card
object. The configuration specifies:

  Prefix              : PM
  Year Segment        : Derived from the system date at time of creation
  Sequence Number     : Configured as a three-digit incrementing counter,
                        reset annually
  Format Assembly     : PM / {YEAR} / {SEQUENCE}
  Example Output      : PM/2026/001, PM/2026/002 ... PM/2026/130

The numbering resets at the beginning of each calendar year. This means
PM/2026/130 and PM/2027/001 can coexist in the system without conflict, as the
year forms part of the unique key. All downstream references in Treasury
Management and Transportation Management store and display this full identifier.

5.5   Custom Logic on the Trade Card
──────────────────────────────────────
Within the Custom Business Objects application, the Custom Logic section allows
business rules to be implemented using a cloud-compliant subset of ABAP
statements. These rules execute at defined trigger points — Before Save,
After Save, On Field Change — and are maintained entirely within the application,
not in an external development environment.

The following custom logic is defined for the Trade Card:

  Trigger: Before Save — New Entry
    —  If Contract Type is "Confirmed Order" and Customer Name is empty,
       raise a validation error: "Customer Name is mandatory for Confirmed Orders."
    —  If Pricing Type is "Fixed" and Fixed Price (USD/MT) is empty,
       raise a validation error: "Fixed Price must be specified."
    —  If Pricing Type is "Floating" and Pricing Formula is empty,
       raise a validation error: "Pricing Formula is required for floating-price
       contracts."
    —  Compute Total Volume (Litres) from Total Volume (MT) using the
       applicable product conversion factor (configurable per Product Type
       in a supporting Code List or lookup).

  Trigger: After Save — New Entry
    —  Set Status to "Active"
    —  Set Total Triggered (MT) to zero
    —  Set Total Unpriced (MT) equal to Total Volume (MT)
    —  Write an entry to the audit log child node recording the creation
       event, the creating user, and the timestamp

  Trigger: On Field Change — Status
    —  If Status changes to "Finance Approved", record Finance Approved By
       and Finance Approved On automatically from the current session
    —  If Status changes to "Closed", record Cargo Closure Date from the
       system date

5.6   Trade Card User Interface
─────────────────────────────────
The Custom Business Objects application generates a standard Fiori list and
object page interface for the Trade Card automatically. This generated UI is
the primary interface for Trading Desk users and provides:

  —  A list view of all Trade Cards with filtering by Status, Contract Type,
     Product Type, and date ranges
  —  A detail page (object page) displaying all field groups in a structured
     layout, with the transaction ID prominently displayed in the page header
  —  Section groupings matching the field model above (Identification, Contract
     Information, Pricing, Trade Finance Summary, Trade Operations Summary)
  —  An audit log section at the bottom showing all status changes and key
     events with timestamps and user names

For users of the Trade Finance and Trade Operations modules, the Trade Card
detail page also acts as a quick-reference summary — they can always open a
Trade Card by its PM/YYYY/NNN reference to see the full contract picture
before proceeding with their specific activities.

6.0   COMPONENT 2 — TRADE FINANCE (TREASURY MANAGEMENT)
================================================================================

6.1   Why Treasury Management
───────────────────────────────
The Treasury Management module in S/4HANA Public Cloud provides a mature,
audit-ready framework for managing financial instruments and cash flows. For
Ardova's Trade Finance function, the module's Transaction Manager component —
specifically its capability to define, track, and manage financial instruments
through their full lifecycle — covers the majority of what Trade Finance needs
without requiring custom development. The investment required here is
primarily in configuration and targeted field extension, rather than building
from scratch.

The match between TRM capabilities and Ardova's Trade Finance requirements is
strong:

  Ardova Requirement                TRM Capability
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Letter of Credit management       Financial transaction with LC product type
  Bank Guarantee management         Financial transaction with BG product type
  Bills for Collection              Financial transaction with BC product type
  Advance Payment tracking          Payment request linked to transaction
  Form M tracking                   Custom field extension on transaction header
  Insurance certificate management  Document attachment capability (GOS / DMS)
  Proforma invoice management       Document attachment + reference field
  Exchange rate capture per trigger  FX transaction linked to trade position
  Finance approval gate             SAP Build Process Automation workflow
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

6.2   Configuration Approach
──────────────────────────────
In S/4HANA Public Cloud, the Treasury Management module is configured through
the Fiori-based configuration applications available within the system. The
primary path is through the "Configure Your Solution" application, where the
relevant configuration activities for Financial Supply Chain Management and
Treasury are organised by topic. This is the correct and supported configuration
channel for Public Cloud — all settings made here are preserved across system
updates and are transported via adaptation transports within the managed system
landscape.

Key configuration activities for this solution:

  PRODUCT TYPES
  Custom product types are defined to represent each financial instrument used
  in Ardova's Trade Finance operations:

    ARDLC  — Ardova Letter of Credit
    ARDBG  — Ardova Bank Guarantee
    ARDBC  — Ardova Bills for Collection
    ARDAP  — Ardova Advance Payment

  For each product type, the system configuration specifies the applicable
  transaction types, flow categories, partner roles, and condition types that
  govern how the instrument behaves through its lifecycle.

  TRANSACTION TYPES PER INSTRUMENT
  Under each product type, specific transaction types define the distinct
  events in an instrument's life:

    For ARDLC (Letter of Credit):
      LC100 — LC Application and Issuance
      LC110 — LC Amendment
      LC120 — LC Utilisation (drawdown upon document presentation)
      LC130 — LC Cancellation

    For ARDBG (Bank Guarantee):
      BG100 — BG Issuance
      BG110 — BG Extension
      BG120 — BG Return or Cancellation

    For ARDBC (Bills for Collection):
      BC100 — BC Presentation
      BC110 — BC Settlement

  BUSINESS PARTNER ROLES
  All counterparties in Trade Finance transactions are maintained as Business
  Partners in S/4HANA. The following partner roles are configured:

    ARDSUP  — Supplier / Seller (the commodity counterparty)
    ARDBNK  — Issuing / Negotiating Bank
    ARDCBN  — Central Bank of Nigeria (for regulatory tracking)

  CONDITION TYPES
  Financial charges associated with trade instruments are captured as
  condition types on each transaction. For Letters of Credit:

    LCCOM  — LC Commission (bank fee, typically a percentage of face value)
    LCSTMP — Stamp Duty
    LCINSUR — Insurance Premium linked to the LC

6.3   Key User Field Extensions on Treasury Management
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Several data points that are specific to Nigerian petroleum trade regulations
are not present in the standard Treasury Management screens. These are added
using the Custom Fields and Logic application in S/4HANA Public Cloud — the
supported extensibility path for adding fields to existing standard objects.

The following custom fields are added to the Financial Transaction object:

  Field Label              Field Type     Applied To         Mandatory
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Trade Card Reference     Text (20)      Transaction Header  Yes
  Form M Number            Text (20)      Transaction Header  Yes (imports)
  Form M Application Date  Date           Transaction Header  Yes (imports)
  Form M Approval Date     Date           Transaction Header  Conditional
  NMDPRA Permit Number     Text (40)      Transaction Header  Yes
  SON Certificate Number   Text (40)      Transaction Header  Yes
  Depot Licence Reference  Text (40)      Transaction Header  Yes
  NAFDAC Permit Number     Text (40)      Transaction Header  Conditional
  Proforma Invoice Ref.    Text (40)      Transaction Header  Yes
  Insurance Certificate    Text (40)      Transaction Header  Yes
  Insurance Coverage Amt.  Amount         Transaction Header  Yes

The Trade Card Reference field is the integration key. When a Trade Finance user
enters a value in this field, a custom logic rule (defined in the Custom Logic
section of the Custom Fields and Logic application) triggers a lookup to the
Trade Card CBO and populates the following fields automatically on the screen:

  —  Supplier Name and Supplier Code
  —  Product Type and Specification
  —  Total Volume (MT) and Total Volume (Litres)
  —  Pricing Type and Pricing Formula
  —  Laycan Start and Laycan End dates
  —  Load Port and Discharge Port
  —  Delivery Terms

This automatic population eliminates re-keying of contract data, ensures
consistency between the Trade Card and the TRM transaction record, and
reduces the risk of discrepancies between what the Trading Desk agreed and
what the Finance team submits to the bank.

6.4   Trade Finance Application Flow
──────────────────────────────────────
The experience for a Trade Finance officer using this system follows a
deliberate sequence. Upon receiving notification that a Trade Card has been
created and activated, the officer opens the Treasury Management application
from the SAP Build Work Zone (the BTP-hosted Fiori Launchpad):

  Step 1 — Locate Transaction
    Open the Manage Financial Transactions application. Create a new financial
    transaction. In the Trade Card Reference field, enter the PM/YYYY/NNN
    identifier received from the Trading Desk. Contract data auto-populates.

  Step 2 — Regulatory Prerequisites
    Confirm that all mandatory regulatory permits are in place before proceeding.
    The NMDPRA Import Permit, SON Certificate, and Depot Licence fields must be
    completed before the system allows the payment instrument section to be saved.
    Document scans of each permit are attached directly to the transaction record.

  Step 3 — Select Payment Mode
    The product type selected (ARDLC, ARDBG, ARDBC, or ARDAP) determines the
    payment instrument workflow. The screen adapts dynamically to show the fields
    relevant to the selected instrument.

    For Letter of Credit:
      The Form M number is entered and the Form M status confirmed. The LC face
      value, LC expiry date, and issuing bank are recorded. A draft LC document
      may be attached for review. Once the Trade Finance team confirms the draft
      is consistent with the contract, the LC is marked as issued.

    For Bills for Collection:
      The Form M is still required for imports. The BC Presentation date,
      presenting bank, and collection reference are recorded.

    For Cash Payment (Local Purchases):
      The payment reference, remittance confirmation number, and payment date
      are recorded. No Form M is required for local purchases.

    For Bank Guarantee:
      The BG reference, face value, issuing bank, and expiry date are recorded.

  Step 4 — Proforma Invoice and Insurance
    The proforma invoice reference and amount are entered. The insurance
    certificate reference and coverage amount are entered, with the certificate
    document attached. These two items are prerequisites for the Finance
    approval action to become available.

  Step 5 — Finance Approval
    When all required documentation is complete, the Trade Finance officer
    submits the transaction for approval. An approval workflow (managed in
    SAP Build Process Automation) routes the record to the designated Trade
    Finance Approver. The approver receives a task notification in their
    Fiori inbox, reviews the record, and confirms or rejects the approval.
    On approval, the Trade Card status is updated automatically to
    "Finance Approved", and the Trade Operations team is notified.

6.5   Floating Price Management Within TRM
────────────────────────────────────────────
For Open Market transactions where the cargo price is determined in stages
through pricing triggers, Treasury Management is used in conjunction with the
FX management capability to record each trigger event as a discrete pricing
action. Each trigger records the ICE Gasoil benchmark rate, the agreed premium,
and the exchange rate prevailing on the trigger execution date.

A custom pricing log — implemented as a sub-node within the Trade Card CBO —
maintains the full history of all triggers, their volumes, prices, and exchange
rates. The TRM transaction for a floating-price cargo remains open until the
final trigger is confirmed, at which point the cumulative product cost is
established and the transaction can be settled.

The distinction between the ICE Gasoil price at each trigger date and the
exchange rate applied at each trigger date is maintained with precision in
the trigger log. These are two separate variable inputs that change
independently, and the system records both explicitly for each tranche of
the cargo that is priced. This level of granularity is essential for accurate
financial reporting and for satisfying any audit inquiry into the final cost
attribution of a given cargo.

  Freight cost management           Freight condition management

  Cargo event logging               Shipment event recording
  Volume data at discharge          Delivery quantity management

7.2   Configuration Approach
──────────────────────────────
Like Treasury Management, Transportation Management configuration in S/4HANA
Public Cloud is performed through the Configure Your Solution application and
the relevant module setup Fiori applications. Key configuration areas include:

  FREIGHT ORDER TYPE
  A custom freight order type ARDCARGO is created to represent an Ardova
  petroleum cargo movement. This type is configured with:
    —  Transport mode: Sea (maritime)
    —  Dangerous goods relevant: Yes (petroleum products classification)
    —  Date profile: Requires Laycan Start, Laycan End, Loading Dates,
       Sailing Date, Arrival Date, Discharge Dates
    —  Completion profile: All mandatory date and volume fields must be
       populated before the freight order can be set to Completed status

  MEANS OF TRANSPORT — VESSEL MASTER
  Vessels are maintained in Transportation Management as Means of Transport.
  A specific means of transport category ARDVSL (Ardova Vessel) is configured
  to represent the vessel fleet. Each vessel in the fleet is set up as an
  individual means of transport record under this category, with its own
  identifier, name, and capacity parameters.

  SERVICE TYPES FOR PETROLEUM CARGO OPERATIONS
  Each category of third-party service provider engaged during cargo operations
  is defined as a distinct service type in TM:

    ARDFEND — Fendering Services
    ARDSTS  — Ship-to-Ship Transfer Operations
    ARDPSRV — Port Surveyor Services
    ARDSTOR — Interim Storage Services
    ARDLAGNT — Load Port Agent
    ARDDAGNT — Discharge Port Agent

  Freight conditions for each service type allow the system to capture budgeted
  service costs at the time the freight order is created, with actual invoiced
  costs updated when service provider invoices are received and processed.

7.3   Key User Field Extensions on Transportation Management
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Custom fields added via the Custom Fields and Logic application extend the
standard Freight Order to capture petroleum-specific operational data:

  Field Label                  Field Type    Applied To        Notes
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Trade Card Reference         Text (20)     Freight Order Hdr  Integration key
  Bill of Lading Number        Text (30)     Freight Order Hdr  Official BL ref
  Bill of Lading Date          Date          Freight Order Hdr  BL issue date
  BL Quantity (MT)             Number        Freight Order Hdr  Official loaded vol
  Shore Quantity (MT)          Number        Freight Order Hdr  Received at depot
  Shore Quantity (Litres)      Number        Freight Order Hdr  Received at depot
  BL-Shore Variance (MT)       Number        Freight Order Hdr  System-computed
  Vessel IMO Number            Text (20)     Freight Order Hdr  IMO registration
  Vessel Owner                 Text (80)     Freight Order Hdr  Owner / operator
  Vessel Flag State            Text (3)      Freight Order Hdr  ISO country code
  Ownership Type               Code List     Freight Order Hdr  Own / Chartered
  Charter Party Reference      Text (30)     Freight Order Hdr  If chartered
  NOR Tendered Date/Time       Date/Time     Freight Order Hdr  Notice of Readiness
  Free Time Allowed (Hours)    Number        Freight Order Hdr  Per contract
  Demurrage Rate (USD/Hour)    Amount        Freight Order Hdr  Per contract
  Demurrage Hours Accrued      Number        Freight Order Hdr  System-computed
  Demurrage Cost (USD)         Amount        Freight Order Hdr  System-computed

  Customs Notification Date    Date          Freight Order Hdr  Regulatory
  PO Number                    Text (10)     Freight Order Hdr  Linked SAP PO
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

As with Trade Finance, the Trade Card Reference field drives automatic population
of contract data from the Trade Card CBO when entered by the Trade Operations
officer.

The BL-Shore Variance and Demurrage Cost fields are system-computed through
custom logic defined in the Custom Fields and Logic application:

  BL-Shore Variance (MT) = BL Quantity (MT) — Shore Quantity (MT)

  Demurrage Hours = MAX( 0, Loading Commencement — (NOR Tendered + Free Time) )
  Demurrage Cost  = Demurrage Hours × Demurrage Rate (USD/Hour)

These computations execute automatically when the relevant input fields are
saved, removing the need for manual calculation and eliminating the risk of
arithmetic errors on high-value items.

7.4   Multi-Point Volume Capture
──────────────────────────────────
Given the financial significance of volume measurements in petroleum cargo
operations — where a single metric tonne of AGO may represent USD 700 to 800
of value — the system maintains a structured volume capture framework as a
sub-node of the Trade Card CBO. Five measurement points are defined, each
with its own date/time, volume in MT, volume in litres, and recording user:

  Capture Point            When Recorded
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Before Loading           Prior to loading operations at load port terminal
  Bill of Lading           At conclusion of loading (Port Surveyor certified)
  Vessel Departure         As vessel sails from load port
  Vessel Arrival           As vessel arrives at discharge port
  Shore Tank Receipt       At conclusion of discharge into depot tanks
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

When the Shore Tank Receipt measurement is saved, custom logic automatically
computes the variance against the Bill of Lading quantity and expresses it as
both an absolute figure (MT) and a percentage. If the variance exceeds 0.5%,
the system raises a notification to the Trade Operations Approver and blocks
the cargo closure action until the variance is formally acknowledged.

7.5   Operational Event Log
─────────────────────────────
A chronological event log sub-node on the Trade Card CBO records all significant
operational milestones with their date, time, and the identity of the recording
officer:

  Laycan Confirmed / NOR Tendered / Free Time Commenced / Loading Commenced /
  Loading Completed / Vessel Sailed / Vessel Arrived at Discharge / Regulatory
  Clearance Obtained / Discharge Commenced / Discharge Completed / Shore Tank
  Receipt Confirmed / Cargo Closure Initiated

This log forms the basis for performance analysis across voyages and provides
the date and time records that service provider invoices are validated against.

7.6   Regulatory Notification Tracking
────────────────────────────────────────
A dedicated notification tracking sub-node records the dispatch and
acknowledgement of mandatory regulatory notifications:

  Regulatory Body    Notification Type       Submission Mode
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Nigerian Navy      Vessel movement          Electronic / Physical
  NMDPRA             Import notification      Electronic / Physical
  Nigeria Customs    Import clearance         Electronic
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Each notification record captures the date sent, the submission mode (the
regulatory environment in Nigeria means some authorities continue to require
physical document submission in addition to or in place of electronic
notification), the acknowledgement date, and the reference number issued by
the receiving authority.

7.7   Real-Time Trading Position View
───────────────────────────────────────
The real-time trading position is a dynamic view of the net balance between
volumes purchased and volumes sold or committed at any given moment. This view
is a critical operational tool for the Trading Desk — it informs trigger
execution decisions, additional procurement needs, and sales commitment capacity.

The position view is implemented as an embedded SAP Analytics Cloud story within
the custom Trade Operations Fiori application deployed on BTP. The story reads
from the Trade Card CBO data in real time, computing:

  Net Position = Total Volume Purchased — Total Volume Sold / Committed

  Positive result → Long position (purchased exceeds sold; price risk if market falls)
  Negative result → Short position (sold exceeds purchased; supply risk)

The dashboard displays:

  —  Current net position by product type (AGO, PMS, DPK, HFO)
  —  Breakdown between triggered volume (priced) and unpriced open quantity
  —  Intra-day position movement chart showing position at each event
  —  Active cargo table: one row per live Trade Card with triggered,
     untriggered, and sold volumes per transaction
  —  Automated threshold alerts when the position crosses into short territory
     or when an unpriced volume is approaching its pricing period end date

The data underlying this view is refreshed on every trigger execution, every
sale entry recorded by the Sales team, and every volume capture event. There is
no end-of-day batch process — position data reflects the current state of the
system at the moment it is viewed.

8.0   DATA FLOW AND CROSS-COMPONENT INTEGRATION
================================================================================

8.1   The Trade Card as the Integration Hub
─────────────────────────────────────────────
The Trade Card CBO is the integration hub of this solution. It does not
replicate data from Treasury Management or Transportation Management — it holds
a summary reference to each, and each module reads from it when a Trade Card
ID is entered. This is a pull-based integration design: the downstream module
requests the contract data it needs from the Trade Card at the moment it is
needed, rather than data being pushed out proactively.

In S/4HANA Public Cloud, the integration between the CBO and the standard
modules is achieved through the OData services that both the CBO and the
standard modules expose. When a user enters a Trade Card ID in either Treasury
Management or Transportation Management, a Custom Logic rule (defined in the
Custom Fields and Logic application for each respective object) makes an OData
call to the Trade Card service, retrieves the relevant contract record, and
populates the dependent fields on the screen. This is an in-system API call
that does not require an external integration platform.

8.2   Cross-System Integration via SAP Integration Suite
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For integration events that cross the boundary between S/4HANA and BTP — such
as publishing a Trade Card creation event so that the BTP-hosted Fiori apps
can respond, or sending workflow notifications, or updating the SAC analytics
data model — SAP Integration Suite on BTP is the platform. Integration flows
(iFlows) are designed for each integration event:

  Integration Event                         iFlow Description
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Trade Card created                        Publishes event to BTP Event Mesh;
                                            triggers notification to Finance team
  Finance approval confirmed                Updates Trade Card status; triggers
                                            notification to Operations team
  Trigger execution confirmed               Updates position master; refreshes
                                            position dashboard data
  Shore volume entered                      Triggers variance calculation check;
                                            alerts approver if threshold exceeded
  Cargo closure confirmed                   Posts final cost to FI/CO; triggers
                                            archive and GL posting workflow
  CBN exchange rate update (daily)          Updates system exchange rate tables
                                            for NGN/USD conversion
  Market price feed (ICE Gasoil)           Updates price reference table for
                                            trigger execution support
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

8.3   Event-Driven Architecture
─────────────────────────────────
Events are published to SAP BTP Event Mesh using the CloudEvents specification.
This decouples the producing system (S/4HANA) from the consuming applications
(BTP Fiori apps, SAC, notification services). Each event carries the Trade Card
ID as its primary payload key, allowing consuming services to retrieve the
full record via OData if additional data is needed.

  Event Topic Pattern: ardova/trade/{component}/{event}

  Examples:
    ardova/trade/card/created
    ardova/trade/finance/approved
    ardova/trade/trigger/executed
    ardova/trade/volume/recorded
    ardova/trade/cargo/closed

10.2  Segregation of Duties
─────────────────────────────
A fundamental control principle enforced throughout the system is that no
single user can both create a record and approve it. This is implemented
through the separation of the Officer and Approver roles, and enforced in the
Custom Logic of the Trade Card CBO and in the SAP Build Process Automation
workflows. The system will reject an approval action submitted by the same
user who created or last modified the record being approved.

and are recognised across all applications without re-authentication. Business
roles assigned in S/4HANA are reflected in the BTP applications through
Principal Propagation.

11.0  WORKFLOW AND APPROVAL DESIGN
================================================================================

All approval workflows in this solution are implemented using SAP Build Process
Automation on BTP. This is the correct workflow mechanism for S/4HANA Public
Cloud, where the classical SAP workflow engine is not the appropriate tool.

Three workflows are defined:

  WORKFLOW 1 — Trade Card Trading Approval
    Trigger    : Trade Card status set to "Pending Trading Approval"
    Task       : Routes to the designated Trading Desk Approver
    Inbox      : Available in the My Inbox application in SAP Build Work Zone
    Actions    : Approve (status → Active) / Reject (status → Draft for revision)
    Escalation : If not actioned within 24 hours, automatic reminder notification

  WORKFLOW 2 — Trade Finance Approval
    Trigger    : All required Finance documentation confirmed as complete
    Task       : Routes to the designated Trade Finance Approver
    Checklist  : Workflow task displays a summary of regulatory permits,
                 instrument details, and document references for review
    Actions    : Approve (Trade Card Finance Status → Finance Approved) /
                 Reject with mandatory rejection note (status → Finance In Progress)
    Effect     : On approval, Operations team receives notification that
                 Finance clearance is confirmed for their cargo

  WORKFLOW 3 — Cargo Closure Approval
    Trigger    : Trade Operations officer requests cargo closure
    Pre-check  : System validates that all volume capture points are recorded,
                 all regulatory notifications are acknowledged, and all
                 operational invoices are entered before allowing the closure
                 request to proceed
    Task       : Routes to Trade Operations Approver and Finance Approver
                 (parallel approval required)
    Actions    : Both approvers must confirm; on dual approval, the cargo is
                 closed, the final cost is posted to FI/CO, and the Trade Card
                 status is set to "Closed"

12.0  EDGE CASES AND RESILIENCE DESIGN
================================================================================

Several business scenarios require specific handling that goes beyond the
standard happy-path flow. These are addressed in the system design as follows:

  OPEN MARKET CARGO — PRICING PERIOD EXPIRY WITH UNTRIGGERED VOLUME
    When the pricing period end date is approached with volume still unpriced,
    the system sends an alert notification to the Trading Desk five days before
    the period closes. On the period end date, the system presents two options:
    execute a final trigger for all remaining open quantity at the current
    benchmark price, or execute a roll-over which extends the pricing period
    by one month at the supplier's roll-over cost. If neither action is taken
    by the end of the period, the Trade Card is flagged for manual intervention.

  BILL OF LADING TO SHORE VARIANCE ABOVE THRESHOLD
    If the computed variance between BL Quantity and Shore Quantity exceeds
    0.5 percent, the system blocks the cargo closure action and raises an
    approval task for the Trade Operations Approver. The approver must
    document the reason for the variance before the block is lifted.
    Variances between 0.2 and 0.5 percent generate a warning notification
    but do not block closure.

  VESSEL ARRIVAL OUTSIDE LAYCAN
    If the NOR Tendered date falls outside the configured Laycan window, the
    system flags the event in the operational log with an "Outside Laycan"
    indicator. The demurrage calculation is adjusted in accordance with the
    relevant contract clause. An alert is sent to the Trading Desk for supplier
    negotiation. The operational officer is required to enter an explanation
    note before the outside-laycan NOR record can be saved.

  LC AMENDMENT AFTER ISSUANCE
    If contract terms change after an LC has been issued, the Trade Finance
    officer creates a new TRM transaction of type LC110 (LC Amendment) linked
    to the original LC transaction. The amendment is documented and subject to
    the same Finance Approval workflow as the original issuance.

  SHORT TRADING POSITION DURING AN ACTIVE TRADING DAY
    When the Sales team records sale volumes that push the net position into
    negative territory (short), the position dashboard immediately reflects
    the short condition and triggers an alert notification to the Trading Desk.
    The system does not prevent the sale from being recorded — the decision
    to allow a short position is a commercial judgement — but it ensures the
    Trading Desk is aware of the exposure without delay.

  CARGO QUALITY REJECTION AT DISCHARGE
    If the Trade Operations officer records a quality failure at the discharge
    port, the cargo closure action is blocked and the Trade Card is placed in
    a "Quality Dispute" status. An escalation notification is sent to the
    Trading Desk and Trade Finance teams for supplier claim management.
    The system preserves all volume and documentation records in their current
    state pending resolution.
